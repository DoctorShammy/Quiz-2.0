How to choose the perfect customer and the perfect buildings

Which customer to address


Customers with large share of their portfolio with same technical preconditions - this makes it easier to scale


Long term digitalisation and sustainability focus - these customers are ready and motivated to receive optimisation solutions


Internal innovation champion - There are usually many internal stakeholders, an internal champion will help you reach and convince them


Customers with portfolio of big buildings - Big buildings have more technical systems and will usually achieve greater optimisation results


It is usually easier to address customers with new innovative solutions where you have good existing relationships



Which buildings to choose

Which attributes to look for


Varied use of the building (day vs night for example) - this means we can shift loads and therefore save more


myCoreAI achieves the best result in buildings with several technical systems, both regarding air handling units and heating circuits, since it has the opportunity to optimize on more individual set-points.


As a natural consequence of the fact that buildings with several technical systems are better, buildings that are slightly larger regarding heated/cooled areas are also good, as they often have more installed units.


Properties that are among the least energy efficient in a portfolio are better to start with. The savings in a building that consumes a lot of energy will naturally be greater, to the benefit of the property owner.


Older buildings are generally better than brand new ones- one reason being that you have no baseline to optimize against in terms of evaluation of the solution in a new building. New buildings are generally more energy efficient from the start as well.


Connected energy meters (main meters) to the BMS is good, but also not a requirement as the energy use can often be obtained directly from energy companies via a third-party service.


This is more of a bonus than necessary, but if the building has existing sensors for temperature (and where applicable CO2) we can probably use them. Otherwise, needed additional sensors are installed (either supplementary sensors or a full set), where Myrspoven uses partners.


Generally good buildings (tier 1)

Offices

Shopping malls

Airports

Universities

This because of their (vast) size their varied use (day vs night) - this means we can shift loads and therefore save more their large number of technical systems which usually means greater savings



Semi-good buildings (tier 2)

Hotels

Schools

Elderly care

Council buildings such as courthouses, and libraries etc

Big Residential buildings

These buildings are not as good as tier 1 buildings, but have proven to achieve good savings because of, for example, their varied use and size.


Other buildings, like train stations, hospitals and logistics facilities, might also be good buildings but need further investigation, like a technical inventory, before proceeding.


What to avoid

In order for Myrspoven's myCoreAI to make the greatest impact and provide the greatest savings, it may be good to avoid buildings with an excessively large proportion of VAV (variable air volume), which are often already well optimized.

Portfolios with small individual buildings

Unusual indoor comfort buildings like, ice rink, swimming pools, factories and other buildings with specific requirements for indoor climate

Customers with unusually hard requirements for IT-security like military facilities

Summary


Go for big buildings with high number of technical systems and buildings using different types of energy types like, heating, cooling and electricity. Avoid ice rinks and swimming pools, aim for the big office buildings to be sure of good savings.
